Chicken broth

Standard Stovetop Chicken Broth

This classic method produces a clear, golden broth perfect for soups, risottos, or sipping.

Photo by Alex Bayev

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken or 2kg of chicken parts
  • 2-3 carrots
  • 2 celery stalks
  • 1 large yellow onion
  • 2-4 cloves of garlic
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • 10 peppercorns
  • fresh parsley
  • fresh thyme
  • 3-4 liters of cold water
  • 1-2 tablespoons of salt

Steps

  1. Wash chicken.
  2. Clean and peel vegetables.
  3. Place the chicken, vegetables and spices into a large stockpot.
  4. Pour cold water until everything is submerged.
  5. Bring the pot to a boil over medium-high heat.
  6. As it begins to boil, a gray foam (scum) will rise to the top. Use a large spoon or mesh skimmer to remove and discard this for a clearer broth.
  7. Reduce the heat to medium-low to maintain a very gentle simmer. Cover the pot and cook for at least 3 hours.
  8. Carefully remove the larger pieces of chicken and vegetables with tongs or a slotted spoon. Pour the remaining liquid through a fine-mesh strainer (lined with cheesecloth for extra clarity) into a large bowl.
